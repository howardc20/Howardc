The Process

The four processes that I used: Corel Draw (CAD), Laser Engraving, 3-D printing, and Woodworking. For Corel Draw, I found a design online and downloaded it onto my computer, then I edited the Bitmap and made the image hairline. I saved it onto a USB flashdrive and inserted it into the laser engraving machine. I configured my settings to an 1/8 inch piece of wood and engraved it. After the laser process was finished, I took my over-sized piece of wood and cut it to my liking using a saw. Now, the big hiccup in my final project process came with the creation of the dog bowl. My goal was to put my dogs' dry dog food into the bowl, but when I returned to the 3-D printer after it was supposedly completed, only one layer of the dog bowl had printed. Just my luck. I huess the filament must have ran out, leaving me with a dog plate, not a bowl. I quickly improvised and decided to transform by 2-D bowl into a frisbee. I attached supports and, suprinsingly, it flies pretty well. While this is not at all what I imagined my final project would look like, I still learned a lot and was able polish some of my skills in the WIC.
