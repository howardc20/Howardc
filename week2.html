The Process

The t-shirt that I designed was inspired by the "WRA Muscical", and I tried to design a shirt that could be marketed to WRA students. I think that the idea is smart, however, the execution was not very good because the ink kind of spread to the white part of the shirt. Unfortunatly, I deleted the original files and did not document the process. For the mug, I used a process called dye sublimation. We printed my WRA cross country logo onto a special piece pf paper and. then, wrapped the paper around the mug and held it in place and heated it. After some time, we took the paper off the mug and submerged it into water. This was a very cool process and it has a few advantages over UV printing, such as you do not an expensive printer to do this process, and, overall, it is a much cheaper process. For popsocket, I used the Roland Versa UV machine. I thought that the Reserve seal would fit well on the circular popsocket. Unfortunatly, I did not save any of the original files.
